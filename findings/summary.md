# Key Findings — Credit Union Auto Loan Delinquency Analysis (2024–2025)

---

## Q1: Which credit unions have the highest total auto loan delinquency?
**Data as of:** 2025-12

Navy Federal Credit Union leads all U.S. credit unions with $413M in total auto 
loan delinquency — nearly 3x the next closest institution. Used vehicle delinquency 
is the dominant driver across every institution in the top 25, suggesting credit 
stress is concentrated in the used car market.

| Rank | Institution | Total Delinquency |
|------|-------------|-------------------|
| 1 | Navy Federal Credit Union | $413,592,852 |
| 2 | Canvas | $140,678,696 |
| 3 | Hughes | $122,684,528 |
| 4 | Suncoast | $117,983,496 |
| 5 | America First | $107,816,434 |

----

## Q2: How has auto loan delinquency trended industry-wide from 2024 to 2025?

Industry-wide auto loan delinquency follows a consistent seasonal pattern across 
both years — Q1 through Q3 show a slow steady rise, with a sharp spike in Q4, 
followed by a reset in Q1 of the following year. Overall delinquency levels have 
remained relatively stable across the full two-year period, suggesting this 
reflects structural seasonal behavior rather than a deteriorating credit environment.

Used vehicle delinquency runs consistently 3x higher than new vehicle delinquency 
across all 8 quarters.

---

## Q3: Which credit unions showed the biggest delinquency increase over the period?

Navy Federal Credit Union showed the largest absolute delinquency increase from 
2024-Q1 to 2025-Q4 at $121.4M, consistent with its position as the largest 
institution in the dataset. More notable is FourLeaf Federal Credit Union, which 
showed a 712% increase over the same period — a significant outlier that warrants 
closer inspection regardless of its absolute size.

The top 25 institutions by absolute increase ranged from $121.4M (Navy Federal) 
down to $11.4M (CommunityAmerica).
